Weapons of Mass Destruction

Biological weapons are used to spread mass disease.

They are inexpensive and difficult to detect once they are deployed (used).

These types of weapons are not widely used by terrorists because they are dangerous to produce.

Examples of biological weapons include anthrax, botulism and smallpox.

Nuclear weapons are used to cause mass devastation to human life and property.

These types of weapons are expensive and difficult to produce.

Their effects include death, radiation burns, poisoning and cancer.

Incendiary weapons are used to start fires.

These types of weapons are easy to make and are most commonly used to destroy property.
